IJKA Founder

Sadashige Kato, 10th Dan

Kato Shihan was born on July 22, 1943 in Kochi Prefecture. He went on to Takushoku University and trained under Masatoshi Nakayama, the founder of Shotokan-ryu. From 1965–1966, he spent time in Germany with Kanazawa Sensei.

A true pioneer of the karate world, Kato Shihan lived in England for more than 50 years, working tirelessly to popularise and develop karate across the globe. He absorbed techniques from four legendary masters:

Masatoshi Nakayama Hirokazu Kanazawa Keinosuke Enoeda Tetsuhiko Asai

Kato Shihan infused what he learned from each of these masters with his own unique techniques, and went on to establish the International Japan Karatedo Association (IJKA). He passed away on April 16, 2020.

"He was a man of humor, love and joy — a great master loved by people all over the world, not only as a legendary karate master, but also as a person."

IJKA Asia & India

Shihan Anil Patil

Shihan Anil Patil is the Head of IJKA Asia and the Chief Instructor of the International Japan Karate Association (India). He holds a 6th Dan in IJKA and a 7th Dan in WKF, bringing an exceptional depth of expertise and international recognition to the organisation.

Under his leadership, IJKA India has grown into a respected national body dedicated to spreading authentic Shotokan karate across the country. The regional administrative headquarters for IJKA India is located in Samta Nagar, Thane, Maharashtra.

A Note From Sensei

Every black belt was once a beginner who showed up.

When people picture a karate dojo, they often imagine something serious, strict, even intimidating. We hope to surprise you. As part of the International Japan Karate-Do Association (IJKA), our training is built on respect — for the art, for your training partners, and for yourself — and respect starts with making people feel welcome.

Whether you're four years old and trying on a gi for the first time, or forty and looking for a new challenge, you'll be greeted the same way: with a bow, a smile, and a place in the line. We train hard, we laugh often, and we celebrate every stripe and belt as a family.

The Dojo Kun

Five promises we make to each other

At the end of every class, we recite the Dojo Kun — a set of guiding principles passed down through generations of Shotokan training. They're less a rulebook and more a reminder of why we're here.

一

Strive to build character

Training is as much about who you become as what you can do.

二

Be sincere and honest

On the mat and off it, your word should mean something.

三

Put in genuine effort

Progress comes from showing up and trying, not from talent alone.

四

Treat others with respect

Every student, from white belt to black belt, deserves courtesy.

五

Keep a level head

Karate gives you the tools to stay calm — use them wisely.

New Here?

What to expect at your first class

No experience, no gi, no problem. Here's exactly what your visit will look like.

1

Arrive a little early

Come in comfortable clothes you can move in.

2

Meet Sensei & bow in

Every class starts with a bow — a simple sign of respect, not a religious gesture. We'll show you exactly what to do.

3

Warm up with the group

Light stretching, footwork drills, and a few laughs as everyone gets moving together.

4

Try your first techniques

Basic stances, blocks, and strikes, broken down step by step and practiced at your own pace.
